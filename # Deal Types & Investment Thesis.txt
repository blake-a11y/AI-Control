# Deal Types & Investment Thesis

> **Venture Portfolio System — Governance Document**
> Defines the investment thesis, deal categories, evaluation criteria, and portfolio rules that guide all opportunity evaluation in this system.
> Referenced by all AI evaluation prompts and the 13-point Blueprint research process.

---

## Investment Thesis Summary

We target **AI-leveraged, recurring-revenue platforms** with clear paths to **$100M+ exits within 5 years**. Every opportunity must score against hard PE metrics (IRR >25%, EBITDA multiple 8–12×, risk-adjusted ROI 5× in 5 years) and fit the combined resources of the partnership (Bird's capital + wealth-tech network; Blake's AI ops + defense/government access + SDVOSB status).

**Core filters:**
- Recurring revenue model (MRR/ARR preferred)
- AI-driven scalability (low marginal cost at scale)
- TAM > $1B with defensible SAM/SOM
- Exit path identified (strategic acquirer or PE roll-up)
- Execution fit: leverages Bird and/or Blake's specific resources
- Cash-flow positive path within 18 months

---

## Required Criteria (Must-Pass)

Every opportunity entering the portfolio must satisfy **all** of the following:

| # | Criterion | Threshold |
|---|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | Recurring revenue model exists or is achievable | MRR/ARR path identifiable |
| 2 | AI scalability is core to the model | Not a bolt-on; structural advantage |
| 3 | Total Addressable Market | TAM > $1B |
| 4 | Exit path is identifiable | PE multiple or strategic acquirer named |
| 5 | IRR (base case) | > 25% |
| 6 | Risk-adjusted return | Positive outcome in pessimistic scenario |
| 7 | Execution fit | Maps to at least 1 partner's resources |

---

## Preferred Criteria (Score Boosters)

These are not required but increase a deal's priority ranking:

- SDVOSB / government contracting angle (Blake's structural moat)
- Phoenix AZ geographic advantage (Blake's physical ops assets)
- Wealth-tech / advisor-channel distribution (Bird's network)
- Cross-portfolio synergy with existing deals
- Low initial capital requirement (< $500K to validate)
- Time-to-revenue under 6 months

## Deal Type Categories

### Category 1: Platform / SaaS
**Description:** Software platforms with subscription revenue and AI-driven automation.
**Examples:** TAMP infrastructure, advisor enablement tools, back-office automation.
**Bird fit:** Deep domain expertise, network distribution.
**Blake fit:** AI buildout, automation, prompt engineering.
**Target metrics:** MRR growth >15% MoM, churn <5%, LTV:CAC >3:1.

### Category 2: Defense / GovTech
**Description:** Dual-use technology for Department of Defense and federal agencies.
**Examples:** AI procurement tools, supply chain tracking, sensor fusion.
**Bird fit:** Compliance infrastructure, institutional sales.
**Blake fit:** Primary — NavalX/DIU/ONR network, SECRET clearance, SDVOSB set-asides.
**Target metrics:** Contract backlog, SBIR/STTR pipeline, non-dilutive funding secured.

### Category 3: Physical Ops / Last-Mile
**Description:** Operationally intensive businesses with AI-optimized logistics.
**Examples:** Robotaxi fleets, warehouse operations, delivery services.
**Bird fit:** Capital deployment, PE-style operational oversight.
**Blake fit:** Primary — Amazon L6 ops background, Phoenix warehouse, F-150.
**Target metrics:** Unit economics per trip/delivery, fleet utilization >65%, regulatory approvals.

### Category 4: Wealth-Tech / FinTech
**Description:** Financial services infrastructure and advisor-facing technology.
**Examples:** UMA platforms, robo-advisory, compliance automation, billing systems.
**Bird fit:** Primary — 30+ years, built and exited a TAMP.
**Blake fit:** AI/automation layer, data pipeline engineering.
**Target metrics:** AUM growth, advisor adoption rate, revenue per advisor.

### Category 5: AI Services / Consulting
**Description:** Service businesses leveraging AI for delivery at scale.
**Examples:** AI business plan consulting, automated research services.
**Bird fit:** Client acquisition via network, quality oversight.
**Blake fit:** Primary — AI tooling, prompt engineering, delivery automation.
**Target metrics:** Revenue per engagement, client acquisition cost, repeat rate.

## Exclusion Zones

The following are explicitly **out of scope** for this portfolio:

| Excluded | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| Insurance / annuity products | Outside Bird's expertise; regulatory complexity |
| Pure consumer apps (B2C) | No recurring revenue path at PE-grade metrics |
| Crypto / blockchain speculative | Does not meet cash-flow-positive requirement |
| Lifestyle businesses | Below $100M exit threshold |
| Capital-intensive manufacturing (>$10M entry) | Exceeds check-size parameters |
| Businesses requiring >50 FTEs in Year 1 | Conflicts with AI-leveraged, low-headcount thesis |

## Evaluation Scorecard Reference

All opportunities are scored using the [Evaluation Scorecard](evaluation-scorecard.md) — 7 criteria, each scored 1–10:

1. **Recurring Revenue** — Clear MRR/ARR path; churn <10%
2. **AI Scalability** — 5-year growth via AI ops; low marginal cost
3. **Market Size** — TAM >$1B; SAM/SOM defensible
4. **Exit Path** — PE multiple 8–12× achievable; acquirer identified
5. **Execution Fit** — Matches Bird + Blake resources
6. **Risk-Adjusted Return** — IRR >25%; pessimistic scenario positive
7. **Portfolio Diversification** — Complements existing portfolio

**Thresholds:**
- **≥ 7.0 average (≥ 49/70):** GO — Full Blueprint research
- **5.0–6.9 average:** HOLD — Revisit after market validation
- **< 5.0 average:** NO GO — Archive

## Portfolio Management Rules

1. **Target portfolio size:** 5–15 active opportunities
2. **Diversification:** No single category >40% of portfolio by capital allocation
3. **Review cadence:** Monthly portfolio review; quarterly deep-dives on top 3
4. **Status progression:** idea-seed → in-analysis → research-complete → decision-pending → in-portfolio (or rejected)
5. **Audit trail:** Every status change, comment, and evaluation is versioned in the repo
6. **No deletions:** History preserved via git. Rejected deals stay archived, not removed.
